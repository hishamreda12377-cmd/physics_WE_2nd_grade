⚡ Unit 1: Forces

Lesson 1: Meaning of Force & Its Units

معنى القوة ووحدات قياسها
💪
1
Introduction START HERE
مقدمة — What is a force?
▾

When you kick a ball, you exert a force. When you sit on a chair, you exert a force. When you push or pull a door, you exert a force too.

A force is a push or pull that results from an interaction between two objects. Forces describe the interactions between an object and its environment.

When there is an interaction, there is a force. When the interaction vanishes, the force disappears.

عندما تركل الكرة، تؤثر بقوة. عندما تجلس على كرسي، تؤثر بقوة. عندما تفتح أو تغلق بابًا، تؤثر بقوة أيضًا.

القوة هي دفع أو سحب ينتج عن تفاعل بين جسمين. القوى تصف التفاعلات بين جسم والبيئة المحيطة به.

عندما يوجد تفاعل، توجد قوة. وعندما يختفي التفاعل، تختفي القوة.

⚽
🌍 Real Life Connection

Think about football: when you kick the ball, you apply a force that changes it from rest to motion. The same happens when you open a fridge door or pull a desk drawer. Force is everywhere!

2
Push & Pull VISUAL
الدفع والسحب — Illustration
▾
3
Definition of Force DEFINITION
تعريف القوة — Force (F)
▾

Force (F): An external effect that affects an object, changing or attempting to change its state, direction, shape, or size.

An action which produces or tends to produce a change in a body's state of rest or motion.

القوة (F): تأثير خارجي يؤثر على الجسم، يغير أو يحاول تغيير حالته، اتجاهه، شكله أو حجمه.

فعل يُنتج أو يميل إلى إنتاج تغيير في حالة السكون أو الحركة لجسم ما.

4
Effects of Force KEY
تأثيرات القوة — 4 main effects
▾

a) Make a body at rest start moving.

b) Stop a moving body or slow it down.

c) Accelerate a moving body.

d) Change direction, shape, and size.

أ) جعل جسم ساكن يتحرك.

ب) إيقاف جسم متحرك أو إبطاؤه.

ج) تسريع جسم متحرك.

د) تغيير الاتجاه، الشكل، الحجم.

🚗
🌍 Real Life — Driving a Car!

a) Press the gas → car moves. b) Press the brakes → car stops. c) Push the pedal harder → car accelerates. d) Turn the steering wheel → direction changes. All forces in action!

5
Types of Forces CLASSIFICATION
أنواع القوى — Contact vs Non-Contact
▾

🤝 Contact Forces (تلامسية)

  • Friction (الاحتكاك)
  • Tension (الشد)
  • Normal Force (العمودية)
  • Air Resistance (مقاومة الهواء)
  • Applied Force (التطبيقية)
  • Spring Force (النابض)

🌌 Non-Contact Forces (غير تلامسية)

  • Gravitational (الجاذبية)
  • Electrical (الكهربائية)
  • Magnetic (المغناطيسية)
🧲
🌍 Real Life

Contact: Writing with a pencil — friction leaves a mark. Non-Contact: A magnet picks up a paperclip without touching it!

6
The Newton — Unit of Force FORMULA
النيوتن — وحدة قياس القوة
▾

The SI unit of force is the Newton (N).

1 Newton = 1 kg·m/s²

Definition: The force required to accelerate a mass of 1 kg at a rate of 1 m/s².

وحدة قياس القوة في النظام الدولي هي النيوتن (N).

1 نيوتن = 1 كجم·م/ث²

تعريف: القوة اللازمة لتسريع كتلة 1 كجم بمعدل 1 م/ث².

📏 Unit of Force
Newton (SI Unit)
1 N = 1 kg · m / s²
🍎
🌍 Imagine This!

An average apple weighs about 1 Newton! When you hold an apple, you feel ~1 N of force. A car needs thousands of Newtons to move!

Lesson 2: Gravitational Force

قوة الجاذبية
🌍
1
What Is Gravity? DEFINITION
ما هي الجاذبية؟
▾

Gravity is the force by which a planet or other body draws objects toward its center. It keeps planets in orbit around the sun.

Anything with mass has gravity. More mass = more gravity. Gravity weakens with distance.

الجاذبية هي القوة التي يجذب بها الكوكب الأجسام نحو مركزه. تبقي الكواكب في مداراتها حول الشمس.

أي شيء له كتلة له جاذبية. كتلة أكبر = جاذبية أكبر. الجاذبية تضعف بزيادة المسافة.

🏀
🌍 Real Life

Why does a ball fall back to Earth instead of floating away? Gravity! Why does Earth stay in orbit around the Sun? Gravity! It's the hidden hero of the universe.

g
2
Newton's Law of Gravitation FORMULA
قانون نيوتن للجذب العام
▾

The gravitational force is directly proportional to the product of masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between centers.

قوة الجاذبية تتناسب طرديًا مع حاصل ضرب الكتلتين وعكسيًا مع مربع المسافة بين المركزين.

🌍 Universal Gravitation
Newton's Law
F = G·m₁·m₂ / r² = m·g

🔑 Key

  • G = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² (universal constant)
  • m₁, m₂ = masses (kg)
  • r = distance between centers (m)
  • The force is mutual: F₁ = F₂ (action-reaction)
3
Solved Example EXAMPLE
مثال محلول
▾
📝 Problem

Two balls, each of mass 7.3 kg, are placed 0.5 m apart (center to center). Calculate the gravitational attraction force between them.

💡 Solution

F = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ × 7.3 × 7.3 / (0.5)² = 1.4×10⁻⁸ N

💡
Notice!

The force is tiny between small objects (1.4×10⁻⁸ N). But it's enormous between celestial bodies like the Earth and Sun!

4
Applications REAL WORLD
تطبيقات الجاذبية
▾

🌌 Gravitation explains:

  • Motion of planets around the Sun
  • Motion of the Moon around Earth
  • Free fall of objects
g

Lesson 3: Centripetal Force

القوة المركزية
🎡
1
Circular Motion DEFINITION
الحركة الدائرية
▾

In uniform circular motion, an object moves in a circular path at constant speed but continuously changing direction. Acceleration is always toward the center.

في الحركة الدائرية المنتظمة، يتحرك الجسم في مسار دائري بسرعة ثابتة المقدار ولكن اتجاه متغير. التسارع نحو المركز دائمًا.

🎢
🌍 Real Life — Amusement Parks!

On a roller coaster loop, you feel pushed toward the center — that's centripetal force! Your body wants to go straight, but the track pushes you into a circle.

2
Key Formulas FORMULA
القوانين الأساسية
▾
🔄 Acceleration
Centripetal Acceleration
ac = v² / r
💪 Force
Centripetal Force
Fc = m·v² / r
3
Factors Affecting Fc KEY
العوامل المؤثرة
▾

📊 Depends on:

  • Mass (m): directly proportional ↑ (truck needs more force than bicycle)
  • Velocity (v): proportional to v² ↑↑ (double speed = 4× force!)
  • Radius (r): inversely proportional ↓ (sharper curves need more force)
🚗
🌍 Why Speed Limits on Curves?

Doubling speed = 4× the centripetal force needed! Sharp curves (small r) also need more force. That's why we slow down on dangerous curves.

4
What If Fc = 0? DANGER
ماذا لو انعدمت؟
▾

If centripetal force suddenly becomes zero, the body moves in a straight line tangent to the circular path (Newton's 1st Law).

إذا انعدمت القوة المركزية فجأة، يتحرك الجسم في خط مستقيم مماس للمسار الدائري (قانون نيوتن الأول).

🛞
🌍 Car Skidding

Oil on the road reduces friction (the centripetal force) → the car skids in a straight line out of the curve. Dangerous!

Lesson 4: Friction & Viscosity

الاحتكاك واللزوجة
🛑
1
What Is Friction? DEFINITION
مفهوم الاحتكاك
▾

Friction is the force that resists motion when two surfaces are in contact. It depends on: pressing force, surface nature, and angle.

الاحتكاك هو القوة التي تقاوم الحركة عندما يتلامس سطحان. يعتمد على: قوة الضغط، طبيعة السطحين، الزاوية.

👟
🌍 Try Walking on Soap!

You'd slip because friction is very low. Friction is what allows you to walk without slipping. Car tires need friction with the road to move and stop safely.

2
Types of Friction CLASSIFICATION
الأنواع
▾

🧊 Static (سكوني)

  • Prevents sliding before motion
  • Strongest type
  • Walking, rock climbing

📝 Sliding (انزلاقي)

  • Acts during sliding
  • Weaker than static
  • Pencil writing, brakes

⚽ Rolling (دحرجي)

  • Acts during rolling
  • Weakest type
  • Wheels, ball bearings
📊
Strength Order:
Static > Sliding > Rolling
3
Viscosity DEFINITION
اللزوجة
▾

Viscosity is the property that resists relative motion of liquid layers. Between two plates (one stationary, one moving), liquid layers move at speeds from 0 to v.

اللزوجة هي الخاصية التي تقاوم الحركة النسبية لطبقات السائل. بين لوحين (ثابت ومتحرك)، تتحرك الطبقات بسرعات من 0 إلى v.

🧴 Viscosity Coefficient
η (eta)
η = F·d / (A·v)

🔬 Units of η

  • N·s/m²  |  kg/(m·s)  |  Pa·s  |  J·s/m³
🛢️
🌍 Lubrication

We use high-viscosity oils for machines — they stay on the parts and don't leak away like water. Lubrication reduces heat and wear!

🔥 Unit 2: Heat

Lesson 1: Temperature & Thermal Energy

درجة الحرارة والطاقة الحرارية
🌡️
1
Heat vs Temperature DEFINITION
الفرق بين الحرارة ودرجة الحرارة
▾

Temperature: Average kinetic energy of molecules.
Heat (Q): Thermal energy transferred due to temperature difference.
Internal Energy: Sum of kinetic + potential energy of molecules.

درجة الحرارة: متوسط الطاقة الحركية للجزيئات.
الحرارة: طاقة تنتقل بسبب فرق الحرارة.
الطاقة الداخلية: مجموع الطاقة الحركية + طاقة الوضع.

☕
🌍 Simple Explanation

A cup of tea at 40°C = its temperature. The heat is the energy that flows from the tea to your hand, making it feel warm!

2
Quantity of Heat FORMULA
كمية الحرارة
▾
🔥 Heat Formula
Quantity of Heat
Qth = m · C · Δt

🔑 Key

  • m = mass (kg)
  • C = specific heat (property of material)
  • Δt = temperature change
  • Q & Δt positive → heat enters, temp rises. Negative → heat leaves, temp drops.
🍳
🌍 Effects of Temperature

1- State change: Ice melts at 0°C (solid → liquid). 2- Expansion: Bridges need joints because they expand in summer and contract in winter!

3
Solved Example APPLICATION
مسألة محلولة
▾
📝 Example: Heating Water

How much heat is needed to raise 2 kg of water from 20°C to 50°C? (Cwater = 4200 J/(kg·K))

Q = m·C·Δt = 2 × 4200 × 30 = 252,000 J = 252 kJ

🫖
🌍 In Real Life

A kettle uses ~2000 W. To heat 2 L of water (same as 2 kg) from 20°C to boiling (100°C): Q = 2 × 4200 × 80 = 672 kJ. At 2000 W, this takes about 5–6 minutes — exactly what you experience every morning!

Lesson 2: Measuring Temperature

قياس درجة الحرارة
🌡️
1
Thermometers & Thermal Equilibrium DEFINITION
مقاييس الحرارة والاتزان الحراري
▾

Thermometers measure temperature based on physical properties that change with temperature. Thermal equilibrium: Two bodies in contact reach the same temperature.

مقاييس الحرارة تقيس درجة الحرارة بناءً على خواص فيزيائية تتغير بتغيرها. الاتزان الحراري: جسمان متلامسان يصبحان بنفس الحرارة.

2
Thermometer Formula FORMULA
معادلة الترمومتر
▾
🌡️ Liquid Thermometer
Temperature from Column Length
t = 100 × (Lt − L0) / (L100 − L0)

🔑 Symbols

  • Lt: length at temperature t
  • L0: length at 0°C (ice point)
  • L100: length at 100°C (steam point)
📝 Example

Mercury length = 15 cm at 0°C, = 40 cm at 100°C. Find t when length = 25 cm.

t = 100 × (25−15)/(40−15) = 40°C

4
Real-Life Thermometers REAL WORLD
مقاييس حرارة من حياتنا
▾
🤒
🌍 Clinical Thermometer

Used to measure body temperature (~37°C). Has a kink (constriction) that prevents mercury from falling back so you can read it after removing!

🌡️
🌍 Outdoor / Room Thermometer

Mercury or alcohol expands in a glass tube. Colored alcohol is often used because it's safer and easier to read — common in homes and gardens.

🔥
🌍 Cooking Thermometer

Used in kitchens to check meat temperature (safe at 75°C+). Also in ovens to make sure the baking temperature is accurate.

📱
🌍 Digital Thermometers

Modern thermometers use thermistors or infrared sensors — no mercury, faster reading, used in schools, hospitals, and even smartphones!

🔥 Unit 2: Heat

Lesson 3: Heat Capacity & Specific Heat

السعة الحرارية والحرارة النوعية
🔥
1
Understanding the Difference DEFINITION
الفرق بين المفهومين
▾

📦 Heat Capacity (qth)

Heat to raise entire body by 1 K

qth = Qth / Δt

Unit: J/K

❌ NOT a physical property — depends on mass

🔬 Specific Heat (Cth)

Heat to raise 1 kg of substance by 1 K

Cth = Qth / (m·Δt)

Unit: J/(kg·K)

✅ IS a physical property — varies by material
🏖️
🌍 Why Does Sand Heat Faster Than Water?

Water has a high specific heat (4186 J/kg·K). Sand has a low specific heat. At the beach: sand is scorching, water is cool!
Analogy: Heat capacity = bucket size. Specific heat = material type (sponge absorbs more than plastic).

2
Solved Example APPLICATION
مسألة محلولة
▾
📝 Example: Comparing Materials

2 kg of water (C = 4200 J/(kg·K)) and 2 kg of copper (C = 385 J/(kg·K)) are each given 10,000 J of heat. Which heats up more?

Δtwater = Q/(m·C) = 10000/(2×4200) ≈ 1.2°C
Δtcopper = 10000/(2×385) ≈ 13°C

Copper heats up 10× more than water with the same energy!

🚗
🌍 Car Engines Use Water

Car radiators use water as coolant because its high specific heat absorbs lots of engine heat without getting too hot itself. Copper fins help release heat quickly because copper has low specific heat but is a great conductor!

Lesson 4: Heat Transfer

انتقال الحرارة
🔥
1
Basics of Heat Transfer DEFINITION
أساسيات انتقال الحرارة
▾

Heat transfer is energy transfer due to temperature difference — always from higher to lower temperature. It may increase internal energy.

انتقال الحرارة هو نقل الطاقة بسبب فرق الحرارة — دائمًا من الأعلى إلى الأقل. قد يزيد الطاقة الداخلية.

2
Three Mechanisms CLASSIFICATION
الآليات الثلاث
▾

🔥 1. Conduction (التوصيل)

  • Direct contact
  • Needs solid medium
  • Metal spoon in hot tea

💨 2. Convection (الحمل)

  • Mass movement of fluid
  • Liquids & gases
  • Air conditioner, boiling water

☀️ 3. Radiation (الإشعاع)

  • Electromagnetic waves
  • No medium needed!
  • Sun warming Earth
🌞
Only Radiation
Needs NO Medium!
Travels through empty space
